## Airplane-Specific ADM Drivers

The frameworks are identical, but the hazards an airplane pilot weighs differ from a PPC's:
- **Stall/spin** on approach or departure — the dominant low-altitude airplane fatal-accident mode; load and bank discipline in the pattern is an ADM matter, not just a stick-and-rudder one.
- **VFR-into-IMC** — a leading cause of fatal GA accidents; conservative ceiling/visibility minimums and a willingness to divert are the defense.
- **Fuel exhaustion/starvation** — airplanes routinely fly longer legs; fuel planning with reserves (14 CFR § 91.151: 30 min day / 45 min night VFR) is a hard ADM gate.
- **Density altitude** on takeoff/climb from hot/high airports — run the POH numbers before committing.
- **Demonstrated crosswind component** — a personal limit to respect (see the airplane weather chapter).

Unlike a PPC, an airplane *can* glide engine-out and reach a chosen field, so an engine-failure decision is about best-glide speed and field selection rather than "immediate landing wherever you are."

## Alcohol and Medication (Airplane / Part 91)

14 CFR § 91.17: no flying within **8 hours of consuming alcohol**, with a blood-alcohol concentration of **0.04% or greater**, or while impaired by any drug. The IMSAFE habit of "no alcohol in the last 24 hours" is a sound conservative personal standard above the regulatory floor.

## Example Personal Minimums — Light Single-Engine Airplane

Starting points for the personal-minimums process; tailor with your CFI. *(Uncertainty flag: these are conservative illustrative values, not regulatory limits.)*

### Lower-Time / Newer Private Pilot
- **Ceiling**: 3,000 ft AGL minimum (well above the 1,000 ft VFR floor)
- **Visibility**: 5 SM minimum
- **Surface wind**: ≤ ~12 kt, with crosswind component ≤ ~half the POH demonstrated crosswind component
- **Gust spread**: ≤ ~10 kt
- **No** forecast icing, embedded thunderstorms, or MVFR/IFR along the route
- **Day VFR only** until night currency and comfort are established

### More Experienced Private Pilot
- **Ceiling**: 2,000 ft AGL minimum
- **Visibility**: 4 SM minimum
- **Surface wind / crosswind**: up to the POH demonstrated crosswind component, with margin for gusts
- **Night** acceptable when current and the route has good lighting/terrain

> As with any category, raise these for unfamiliar airports, fatigue, a passenger, or stacked risk factors — and never relax them on the day of the flight to justify going. If any minimum is exceeded, it's a no-go.

## Airplane Scenario Notes

- **Marginal ceiling/visibility** trending toward MVFR → divert or land early; do not press toward IMC as a VFR pilot.
- **Run-up mag drop beyond POH limits, or rough engine** → do not depart; investigate.
- **Engine failure in cruise** → pitch for best glide, pick the best field within glide range, run the engine-failure checklist (this airplane-specific procedure is in the airplane emergencies chapter).

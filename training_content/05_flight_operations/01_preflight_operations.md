# Flight Operations for Powered Parachutes
## From Preflight to Postflight - Complete Operational Procedures

---

## Introduction

This module covers the complete operational procedures for powered parachute flight, from pre-flight inspection through post-flight procedures. Mastery of these procedures is essential for safe, efficient, and enjoyable flying.

**Core Principle**: Consistent, thorough procedures prevent accidents. Develop habits that ensure safety every time you fly.

---

## Pre-Flight Planning

### Weather Evaluation

**24 Hours Before Flight**:
- Check extended forecast
- Note frontal systems approaching
- Review temperature trends
- Check wind forecasts

**Morning of Flight** (2-3 hours before):
- Call 1-800-WX-BRIEF or check online
- Review current METAR for nearby airports
- Check TAF if available
- Review radar for precipitation
- Check for TFRs (Temporary Flight Restrictions)
- Note sunrise/sunset times

**On-Site Evaluation** (before setup):
- Observe current wind conditions
- Look at sky (clouds, visibility)
- Check temperature
- Feel the air (smooth or turbulent)
- Make final go/no-go decision

**Go/No-Go Criteria**:
- ✓ Wind within personal limits (5-10 mph max)
- ✓ No gusts or minimal gusts only
- ✓ Visibility adequate (3+ miles)
- ✓ No approaching weather
- ✓ No TFRs in area
- ✓ Pilot feels well and prepared
- ✗ ANY doubt = NO GO

### Flight Planning

**Determine**:
1. **Takeoff/Landing Direction**: Into the wind
2. **Flight Area**: Where you'll operate (stay in glide distance of fields)
3. **Duration**: Based on fuel and conditions
4. **Altitude**: Operating altitude (typically 500-1,500 AGL)
5. **Passengers**: Solo or dual (if qualified)

**Weight and Balance**:
- Calculate total weight
- Verify within limits for your wing
- Adjust trim if needed for pilot weight
- Consider fuel weight (6 lbs/gallon)

**Fuel Planning**:
- Calculate fuel needed (plus reserve)
- Typical consumption: 4-5 gallons/hour
- Minimum reserve: 1 gallon
- Visual check of fuel level
- Know your endurance

## Pre-Flight Inspection

### Systematic Approach

Use the same pattern every time. Example: Start at nose wheel, proceed clockwise around aircraft

**Inspection Checklist** (Detailed):

#### **1. Nose Wheel Area**
- [ ] Tire pressure correct (8-12 psi)
- [ ] No cuts, bulges, or damage to tire
- [ ] Wheel bearings tight, no play
- [ ] Brake functional (if equipped)
- [ ] Steering linkage secure and operating
- [ ] Centering springs intact

#### **2. Left Front Area**
- [ ] Steering bar secure and moving freely
- [ ] Steering lines in good condition
- [ ] Trim system secure, set correctly
- [ ] No tangles in lines

#### **3. Instrument Panel**
- [ ] All instruments functioning
- [ ] Switches in OFF position (mags)
- [ ] Key in ignition or secured
- [ ] Warning lights operational (if equipped)
- [ ] EIS powered on and functional

#### **6. Left Rear Area**

- [ ] Rear seat belt intact

- [ ] No loose or missing hardware

#### **7. Left Wheel**
- [ ] Tire pressure correct
- [ ] Tire condition good

- [ ] Rear throttle secure (if equipped)

#### **16. Right Wheel**

- [ ] Tire condition good

- [ ] Suspension functional

#### **17. Right Riser and Attachment**

#### **18. Right Front Area**
- [ ] Front seat secure (right side)
- [ ] Throttle lever moves smoothly

#### **19. Nose Wheel (Right Side)**
- [ ] Steering linkage secure

#### **20. Final Walk-Around**

## Engine Starting Procedures

### Warm Engine Start (Recently Run)

1. **Clear Area**: Ensure prop area is clear
2. **Mags OFF**: Verify both mag switches OFF
3. **Master On**: Turn on master switch (if equipped)
4. **Prime**: 2-3 shots of primer
5. **Throttle**: Crack open slightly (1/4")
6. **Clear Prop**: Loudly announce "CLEAR PROP!"
7. **Visual Check**: Look behind and around aircraft
8. **Start**: Turn key to start
9. **Release Key**: Release as soon as engine starts
10. **Idle**: Let engine idle (2,000 RPM)

### Cold Engine Start

1-7. **Same as warm start**
8. **Prime More**: Use more primer (4-6 shots)
9. **Choke**: Apply choke if equipped
10. **Start**: Turn key, may need longer cranking
11. **Catch**: As engine fires, adjust throttle/choke
12. **Warm Up**: Let engine warm at higher idle (2,500 RPM)

### Flooded Engine Start

**Symptoms**: Engine won't start, smells of fuel

**Procedure**:
1. **Stop Trying**: Don't keep cranking
2. **Clear**: Wait 2-3 minutes
3. **No Prime**: Don't use primer
4. **Full Throttle**: Open throttle fully
5. **Crank**: Turn over engine briefly
6. **Throttle Back**: Return to idle position
7. **Try Again**: Attempt normal start

**Alternative**: Use primer to flood more, shuts engine down, then wait and clear

## Engine Warm-Up and Checks

### Warm-Up Procedure

**Duration**: Minimum 5 minutes, until:
- Coolant temperature 140°F or higher
- EGT stabilized
- Engine running smoothly

**Process**:
1. **Idle**: Start at low idle (2,000 RPM)
2. **Gradually Increase**: Bring to 3,000-3,500 RPM
3. **Vary RPM**: Don't run at constant RPM
4. **Monitor Temps**: Watch all temperatures
5. **Check Voltage**: Should be 13.5-14.5V

### Magneto Check

**Procedure**:
1. **Secure Aircraft**: Hold brake or have help
2. **RPM**: Run at 3,500 RPM
3. **Left Mag Off**: Turn off left mag switch
4. **Note Drop**: RPM should drop 100-300
5. **Left Mag On**: Turn back on immediately
6. **Right Mag Off**: Turn off right mag switch
7. **Note Drop**: Should be similar to left
8. **Right Mag On**: Turn back on
9. **Idle**: Return to idle

**Acceptable**: Both mags drop similar amount (100-300 RPM)
**Unacceptable**:
- One mag drops more than 300 RPM
- One mag drops much more than other
- Engine won't run on one mag
- **DO NOT FLY** if mag check fails

### Full Power Check

**Purpose**: Verify engine develops full power

**Procedure**:
1. **Secure Aircraft**: Tie down or have strong helper hold
2. **Safety**: Sit in seat, hands on mag switches
3. **Verify Temps**: Engine at operating temperature
4. **Full Throttle**: Advance throttle to maximum
5. **Check RPM**: Should reach 6,350-6,450 RPM (typical)
6. **Check Temps**: Watch EGT, CHT, coolant
7. **Duration**: 5-10 seconds only
8. **Idle**: Return to idle immediately

**Acceptable**:
- RPM reaches expected level (per propeller pitch)
- All temperatures in normal range
- Engine runs smoothly
- No unusual sounds or vibrations

**Unacceptable**:
- Low RPM (indicates pitch problem or engine issue)
- High temperatures
- Rough running
- Unusual sounds
- **DO NOT FLY** if full power check fails

## Wing Layout and Inflation

### Wing Layout Procedure

**Site Selection**:
- Flat, clear area
- Into the wind
- Free of obstacles
- Adequate takeoff distance
- Escape routes available

**Layout Methods**:

#### **Stacked Method** (High Wind)
1. Place bag behind aircraft, label facing away
2. Remove wing from bag
3. Stack cells neatly, one on top of another
4. Lead trailing edge away from aircraft
5. Weight if necessary
6. Remove line socks
7. Check for twists/tangles

#### **Spread Method** (Low Wind)
1. Place bag behind aircraft
2. Remove wing, spread in horseshoe shape
3. Separate cells
4. Arrange neatly
5. Remove line socks
6. Check lines

### Line Check

**Critical Process** - Take Your Time!

1. **Start at Risers**: Begin where lines attach to aircraft
2. **Follow Each Line**: Trace every line from riser to wing
3. **Check for**:
   - Tangles
   - Twists
   - Line overs (line over top of wing)
   - Damage
   - Proper routing
4. **Steering Lines**: Verify routing through pulleys
5. **Both Sides**: Complete both left and right
6. **No Slack**: Take up slack in risers/steering lines
7. **Final Check**: Walk through one more time

**Common Problems**:
- Line over top of wing
- Lines tangled with each other
- Lines twisted around riser
- Steering line routed incorrectly
- Lines caught on equipment

**If Problems Found**: Fix before attempting inflation!

### Wing Inspection

**Before Inflation**:
- Walk length of wing
- Check fabric condition
- Look for tears or damage
- Verify cells will inflate
- Check leading edge openings
- Verify trailing edge condition

## Ground Operations and Taxiing

### Getting In and Secured

**Sequence**:
1. **Passenger First** (if applicable):
   - Brief on procedures and safety
   - Assist into seat
   - Secure seat belt
   - Connect intercom if equipped
   - Brief on propeller danger
2. **Pilot**:
   - Secure any loose items
   - Get in seat
   - Fasten seat belt/harness
   - Adjust as needed
   - Connect intercom
3. **Final Check**:
   - Both secure
   - Steering lines not slack
   - Clear of obstacles
   - Ready to proceed

### Taxiing

**Controls**:
- **Steering**: Steering bar controls direction
  - Push right = turn left
  - Push left = turn right
- **Brake**: Hand lever (if equipped)
- **Throttle**: Control speed with throttle
- **Wing**: Keep wing secured or have helper

**Taxiing Technique**:
1. **Start Slowly**: Idle or slightly above
2. **S-Turns**: Make gentle S-turns to check ahead
3. **Avoid Obstacles**: Give wide clearance
4. **Speed**: Fast walk only
5. **Brake**: Use sparingly (limited effectiveness)
6. **Wing**: Don't let wing inflate (keep collapsed)

**Safety**:
- Watch for people
- Check wind direction
- Be ready to stop
- Don't taxi with wing inflated (can't control)

### Wing Inflation (Kiting)

**Purpose**: Verify wing will inflate properly before committing to takeoff

**Procedure**:
1. **Position**: Lined up into wind
2. **Check**: Area clear, escape routes available
3. **Power**: Smoothly apply power (3,000-4,000 RPM)
4. **Wing Rises**: Wing begins to inflate and rise
5. **Control**: Make small steering corrections as needed
6. **Overhead**: Wing comes overhead
7. **Check**: Visually check wing looks good
8. **Decision**:
   - Good: Proceed with takeoff
   - Bad: Abort, lower power, collapse wing

**What to Look For**:
- ✓ Wing inflates evenly
- ✓ Wing comes overhead smoothly
- ✓ Wing takes on proper shape
- ✓ No lines visible (means they're under wing)
- ✗ Uneven inflation
- ✗ Lines visible
- ✗ Wing distorted shape
- ✗ Wing oscillating or unstable

**Abort Criteria**:
- Lines visible from pilot position
- Uneven inflation
- Wing not overhead
- Feeling that something is wrong

## Takeoff

### Final Checks Before Takeoff

**BEFORE Applying Power**:
- [ ] Into the wind
- [ ] Runway/area clear
- [ ] Escape routes available
- [ ] Seat belt secure
- [ ] Loose items secured
- [ ] Steering lines not slack but not tight
- [ ] Trim set correctly
- [ ] Engine temperatures normal
- [ ] Mental preparation complete

### Normal Takeoff

**Sequence**:
1. **Line Up**: Straight into wind
2. **Feet Forward**: Steering bars neutral (slightly forward)
3. **Power**: Smoothly advance to full throttle
4. **Wing Inflation**: Wing begins to rise
5. **Minor Corrections**: Small steering inputs only
6. **Overhead Check**: Glance up - wing looks good?
7. **Accelerate**: Continue accelerating
8. **Feel Light**: Cart begins to feel light
9. **Liftoff**: Aircraft gently lifts off (don't force it)
10. **Climb**: Establish positive climb

**Speeds** (typical):
- Rotation: ~25 mph
- Liftoff: ~28 mph
- Climb: ~30 mph

**Common Errors**:
- Excessive steering inputs during inflation
- Not checking wing before committing
- Trying to force aircraft off ground
- Climbing too steeply after liftoff
- Not maintaining directional control

### Aborted Takeoff

**Reasons to Abort**:
- Wing doesn't inflate properly
- Lines visible
- Wing unstable
- Obstacle appears
- Wind shift
- Engine problem
- "Bad feeling"

**Procedure**:
1. **Decision**: Make abort decision early!
2. **Power**: Close throttle immediately
3. **Mag Switches**: Turn off if time allows
4. **Steering**: Full forward (collapses wing)
5. **Brake**: Apply brake
6. **Straight**: Maintain directional control
7. **Stop**: Come to complete stop
8. **Assess**: Determine problem before trying again

**Critical**: Better to abort unnecessarily than continue bad takeoff!

## Climb and Cruise

### Climb

**Normal Climb**:
- Full power (6,350-6,450 RPM)
- Airspeed: 28-32 mph
- Rate: 300-800 fpm (varies with weight and conditions)
- Monitor temperatures
- Straight ahead or gentle turns only

**Climb Technique**:
- Maintain steady airspeed
- Small power adjustments as needed
- Avoid steep turns (requires more power)
- Level off before reaching desired altitude

**Leveling Off**:
1. **Power Back**: Reduce power as approaching altitude
2. **Accelerate**: Allow speed to build
3. **Trim**: Adjust trim if needed
4. **Stabilize**: Establish cruise flight

### Cruise

**Cruise Settings**:
- Power: 5,000-5,800 RPM (reduced from climb)
- Airspeed: 25-30 mph typical
- Altitude: 500-1,500 AGL typical
- Monitor: All instruments, traffic, weather

**In-Flight Checks** (Every 10-15 Minutes):
- Engine gauges (RPM, temps)
- Fuel quantity
- Electrical system (voltmeter)
- Location (where am I?)
- Weather conditions
- Traffic (other aircraft)

**Efficiency Tips**:
- Higher altitude = better efficiency (less drag)
- Reduced power = better fuel economy
- Smooth air = better performance
- Maintain coordinated flight (minimize drag)

## Turns

### Normal Turns

**Technique**:
1. **Look**: Check turn direction for traffic
2. **Input**: Apply steering on inside of turn
3. **Bank**: Aircraft banks toward turn
4. **Coordinate**: May need opposite steering slightly
5. **Monitor**: Maintain altitude, airspeed
6. **Rollout**: Release pressure as approaching desired heading
7. **Neutralize**: Return to wings-level flight

**Turn Rate**:
- Gentle turns: 10-15° bank
- Standard rate: 20-30° bank
- Steep turns: Up to 60° bank (avoid if possible)

**Altitude Control**:
- Pulling on toggles helps maintain altitude in turn
- May need slight power increase
- Watch airspeed (don't slow too much)

### Steep Turns

**Definition**: Greater than 45° bank

**Caution**: Requires skill and practice
- Increased load factor
- Increased sink rate
- Reduced stall margin
- Not recommended for students

**If Performing Steep Turns**:
- Adequate altitude (1,000+ AGL)
- Smooth air only
- Start with shallower bank, increase gradually
- Significant power increase needed
- Pull on toggles to maintain altitude
- Monitor airspeed carefully
- Exit before uncomfortable

## Descent

### Types of Descents

#### **Powered Descent**
- Reduce power below cruise
- Wing maintains normal attitude
- Gentle descent rate (100-300 fpm)
- Good control and stability
- Preferred for normal approach

#### **Idle Descent (Glide)**
- Reduce power to idle
- Steeper descent (300-500 fpm)
- Faster descent for losing altitude
- Higher sink rate
- Used for rapid descent or practice

#### **Maximum Performance Descent**
- Full brakes (both steering lines)
- Very steep descent
- High sink rate (>500 fpm)
- Short forward distance traveled
- Used for confined area landing or obstacle clearance

### Descent Technique

**Powered Descent**:
1. **Clear Below**: Check landing area
2. **Power**: Reduce throttle gradually
3. **Attitude**: Nose lowers slightly
4. **Airspeed**: Maintain safe airspeed
5. **Rate**: Monitor descent rate
6. **Adjust**: Power for desired rate

**Considerations**:
- Don't descend too fast (passenger comfort, control)
- Maintain safe airspeed
- Watch for turbulence (often worse at lower altitudes)
- Plan to level off before obstacle/ground

## Traffic Pattern and Landing

### Traffic Pattern

**Standard Pattern** (left-hand):
1. **Upwind**: Takeoff leg, straight out
2. **Crosswind**: 90° left turn at 300-500 AGL
3. **Downwind**: Parallel to runway, opposite direction
4. **Base**: 90° left turn, perpendicular to runway
5. **Final**: Aligned with runway, descending

**Pattern Altitude**: 500-800 AGL typical

**Considerations for PPCs**:
- Lower and tighter than airplane pattern
- Stay within glide distance of landing area
- Watch for airplanes (they're faster)
- Maintain visibility with other traffic

### Landing Approach

**Planning**:
- Choose landing area (into wind)
- Plan pattern to arrive on final aligned with wind
- Altitude and distance to allow stabilized approach

**Final Approach**:
1. **Aligned**: On centerline (into wind)
2. **Stabilized**: Constant descent, steady airspeed
3. **Airspeed**: Maintain safe airspeed (not too slow)
4. **Power**: Use power to control descent rate
5. **Obstacles**: Clear any obstacles
6. **Approach Path**: Aim for touchdown point

**Approach Speeds**:
- High approach: 28-30 mph
- Normal approach: 25-28 mph
- Short field: 22-25 mph (with care)

### Landing Flare and Touchdown

**Landing Sequence**:

1. **Short Final** (50-100 feet):
   - Maintain approach speed
   - Aim for touchdown point
   - Make final power adjustments

2. **Roundout** (15-20 feet):
   - Begin reducing power
   - Gradually pull on both steering lines (flare)
   - Nose rises, sink rate decreases

3. **Touchdown** (5-10 feet):
   - Continue gradual flare
   - Reduce power to idle (before touchdown)
   - Gentle touchdown on main wheels
   - Nose wheel touches gently

4. **After Touchdown**:
   - **Immediately**: Full forward on steering bars
   - **Pull In**: Pull steering lines in by hand
   - **Collapse Wing**: Wing collapses behind aircraft
   - **Stop**: Apply brake if needed
   - **Mags Off**: Turn off mag switches

**Landing Flare**:
- **Too Early**: Aircraft balloons, stalls from height
- **Too Late**: Hard touchdown
- **Just Right**: Gentle, controlled touchdown

**Critical**: Collapse wing immediately after touchdown to prevent wing from falling on hot engine/propeller or dragging aircraft

### Short Field Landing

**When Needed**:
- Confined area
- Obstacles on approach
- Limited landing area

**Technique**:
1. **Steep Approach**: Use idle or low power
2. **Slow Speed**: Approach at minimum safe speed
3. **Full Brakes** (if needed): For very steep approach
4. **Flare Late**: Wait until lower to flare
5. **Touch Down Firmly**: Accept firmer touchdown
6. **Collapse Immediately**: Full forward, pull lines

**Caution**: Requires skill and practice, higher risk of hard landing or stall

### Go-Around (Abandoned Approach)

**When to Go Around**:
- Unstabilized approach
- Too fast or too slow
- Wrong direction or alignment
- Obstacle appears
- Traffic conflict
- "Bad feeling"

**Procedure**:
1. **Decision**: Make early!
2. **Power**: Full power immediately
3. **Climb**: Establish positive climb
4. **Steering**: Release any brake pressure
5. **Fly**: Fly away from ground
6. **Pattern**: Re-enter pattern for another approach

**Remember**: It's ALWAYS OK to go around. Don't try to salvage a bad approach!

## Post-Flight Procedures

### Shutdown

**After Landing and Stopping**:
1. **Mags Off**: Turn off both mag switches
2. **Engine Stops**: Engine shuts down
3. **Master Off**: Turn off master switch
4. **Key Out**: Remove key from ignition
5. **Sit**: Remain seated until prop stops
6. **Secure**: Set brake if equipped

### Post-Flight Inspection

**Walk Around**:
- Check for damage
- Look for leaks
- Check tires
- Inspect wing briefly
- Collect any dropped items

### Aircraft Securing

**Wing**:
1. **Pack Wing**: Fold and pack into bag
   - Start at trailing edge
   - Fold sides in
   - Roll or stack toward leading edge
   - Secure lines
   - Place in bag
2. **Secure to Aircraft**: Strap bag in rear seat or secure elsewhere

**Aircraft**:
1. **Tie Down**: If leaving aircraft outside
2. **Covers**: Install prop cover, exhaust plug, carb covers
3. **Fuel Cap**: Verify tight
4. **Fuel Up**: Refill fuel tank
5. **Oil**: Check and refill oil tanks
6. **Logbook**: Make entry (if required)
7. **Secure Area**: Clean up, check nothing left behind

### Fueling

**Procedure**:
1. **Ground Aircraft**: Ensure electrical path to ground
2. **No Smoking**: No open flames or smoking within 50 feet
3. **Fill Tank**: Use clean fuel only
4. **Don't Overfill**: Leave room for expansion
5. **Cap Secure**: Tighten fuel cap
6. **Fill Oil**: Top off oil tank (every time you fuel)
7. **Clean Spills**: Wipe up any spilled fuel
8. **Check Sample**: Drain gascolator, check for water

## Common Errors and How to Avoid Them

### Takeoff Errors

**Error**: Excessive control inputs during inflation
**Fix**: Keep feet slightly forward, make small corrections only

**Error**: Not checking wing before committing to takeoff
**Fix**: Glance up, verify wing overhead and properly inflated

**Error**: Forcing aircraft off ground
**Fix**: Be patient, let aircraft fly when ready

### Flight Errors

**Error**: Flying too slow
**Fix**: Monitor airspeed, maintain safe margin above stall

**Error**: Inadequate visual scanning
**Fix**: Continuous head movement, check all quadrants

**Error**: Fixating on instruments
**Fix**: Look outside most of the time

### Landing Errors

**Error**: Too fast on approach
**Fix**: Use power reduction and/or brakes to slow

**Error**: Flaring too high
**Fix**: Wait until 5-10 feet to begin full flare

**Error**: Not collapsing wing after landing
**Fix**: Immediate full forward steering, pull lines by hand

## Summary

Safe powered parachute operations require:
1. **Thorough Planning**: Weather, weight, fuel
2. **Complete Inspection**: Every time, same way
3. **Proper Procedures**: Follow proven techniques
4. **Constant Monitoring**: Stay aware and alert
5. **Conservative Decisions**: When in doubt, don't

**Remember**: Every flight should be conducted as though an instructor were watching. Develop good habits that ensure safety and success every time you fly.
